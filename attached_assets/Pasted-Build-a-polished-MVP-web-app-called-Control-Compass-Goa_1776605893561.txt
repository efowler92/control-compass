Build a polished MVP web app called **Control Compass**.

## Goal

Create a small-business cybersecurity readiness checker that feels professional, practical, and portfolio-worthy. The app should help a user answer a short assessment, calculate a readiness score, identify risk areas, and generate a prioritized action plan.

This is an educational cyber/compliance-prep tool, not legal advice.

## Product concept

The app should let a user complete a short security assessment for their business or organization. Based on their answers, it should produce:

* an overall readiness score from 0 to 100
* a risk level: Low, Moderate, or High
* category scores
* a list of the most important next steps
* a clean dashboard-style results page

## Target user

Small business owners, solo founders, freelancers, or very small teams who need basic cybersecurity and compliance readiness guidance in plain English.

## Tech stack

Use:

* React
* Tailwind CSS
* clean component-based structure
* local state only for MVP
* no authentication yet
* no database yet

Build it so it runs cleanly in Replit.

## App structure

Create these sections/pages:

### 1. Landing page

Include:

* app name: Control Compass
* short tagline like: “A simple cybersecurity readiness check for small businesses”
* short description of what it does
* button to start assessment

### 2. Assessment form

Create a user-friendly questionnaire with around 12 yes/no questions.

Use these questions:

1. Is multi-factor authentication enabled for business email accounts?
2. Are company devices encrypted?
3. Are regular backups performed?
4. Are backups tested periodically?
5. Are systems and software patched on a schedule?
6. Do employees use unique user accounts instead of shared logins?
7. Are administrative privileges restricted to only those who need them?
8. Is endpoint protection or antivirus installed on business devices?
9. Is there a documented incident response process?
10. Do employees receive phishing or security awareness training?
11. Is sensitive customer or business data stored securely?
12. Are third-party vendors reviewed before being given access to sensitive data?

Make the form clean and modern, with clear labels and simple answer controls.

### 3. Scoring logic

Use weighted scoring. More important missing controls should reduce the score more heavily.

Use this weighting model:

* MFA enabled: 15 points
* Device encryption: 10 points
* Regular backups: 15 points
* Backup testing: 8 points
* Patch management: 10 points
* Unique user accounts: 8 points
* Restricted admin privileges: 10 points
* Endpoint protection: 8 points
* Incident response process: 8 points
* Security awareness training: 4 points
* Secure data storage: 8 points
* Vendor review process: 6 points

Total should equal 100.

If the answer is “Yes,” award the points.
If “No,” award zero points.

### 4. Risk level rules

Map the final score to:

* 80–100 = Low Risk
* 60–79 = Moderate Risk
* 0–59 = High Risk

### 5. Category breakdown

Group questions into these control domains and show domain-level scores:

* Access Control

  * MFA
  * Unique accounts
  * Restricted admin privileges
* Data Protection

  * Device encryption
  * Secure data storage
* Resilience

  * Backups
  * Backup testing
  * Incident response
* Operational Security

  * Patch management
  * Endpoint protection
  * Security awareness training
* Third-Party Risk

  * Vendor review

### 6. Results page

After submission, show:

* overall readiness score
* risk level badge
* short explanation of what the score means
* domain/category score cards
* top 5 priority actions based on missing controls
* a short disclaimer that the tool provides educational guidance, not legal or regulatory advice

## Recommendation engine

Generate recommendations dynamically based on “No” answers.

Use practical plain-English recommendations like:

* Enable multi-factor authentication for all business email and admin accounts
* Turn on device encryption for laptops and mobile devices
* Create regular backups and verify they can be restored
* Establish a recurring patch schedule for systems and applications
* Eliminate shared logins and assign each user a unique account
* Limit administrator privileges to only essential personnel
* Install and maintain endpoint protection on all devices
* Create a basic incident response checklist for cyber events
* Provide phishing and security awareness training
* Review vendors before giving them access to sensitive systems or data

Rank the most important recommendations first based on control weight.
